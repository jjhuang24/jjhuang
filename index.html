About Me

Hello! I am currently pursuing a PhD degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Purdue University, where I work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Video and Image Processing Laboratory (VIPER) supervised by Professor Fengqing Maggie Zhu. My research ranges from video compression techniques to anomaly detection in images. Prior to this, I earned my Master's degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering with a thesis in 2025 and a Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering from Purdue University in 2024.

During these roles, I worked on developing software solutions, optimizing workflows, and gaining hands-on experience in real-world engineering applications.

Outside of academics and work, I enjoy rock climbing, enjoying time with friends, playing musical instruments, and continuously finding new ways to challenge myself.
